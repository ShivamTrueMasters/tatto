Many of the bird’s traits are things that human beings aspire to. Most of all, the freedom of flight symbolize the freedom that human beings want to change. Inventors like Leonardo Da Vinci focused on ways that man could soar to the same heights as the birds do.

The Bird Tattoo Meaning

While many bird tattoos have common meanings, the meaning can vary depending on the type of bird and the type of tattoo that you choose.

  • Nobility
  • Beauty
  • Spiritual Guidance
  • Elegance
  • Completely Freedom
  • Knowledge and Intelligence
  • Peace
  • Family
  • Motherhood
  • Love

Phoenix Tattoo Meaning

Out of the many bird species, the Phoenix is one of the most common tattoos. In mythology, the Phoenix was said to die in a fiery flame and then rise again from the ashes. As such, the Phoenix was a symbol of rebirth and transformation. Early Christians used the Phoenix to represent the resurrection of Jesus Christ. The longevity of the Phoenix was also symbolic for the way that Christians would survive persecution and destruction to rise from the proverbial ashes.

Dove Tattoo Meaning

For thousands of years, the dove has been a symbol of love and peace. Long ago, the dove was a symbol of the Canaanite mother goddess, Asherah.

Thunderbird Tattoos

The Thunderbird is a creature of myth and legend. While inspired by the eagle, this creature was known for being a spiritual guide, a fierce warrior, a nurturing figure and a divine creator. When used in a tattoo, the Thunderbird can represent dominance, nobility, power, strength and honor.

Bird of Paradise Tattoo

The bird of paradise is the diva of the avian kingdom. This bird is exceptionally outgoing and beautiful. These small animals are known for their elegance, grace and beauty.

Falcon Tattoo

It often represents the rising sun and new beginnings. With their focus and intelligence, the falcon is symbolic of man’s ability to remain committed to the goal despite any obstacles that are encountered.
